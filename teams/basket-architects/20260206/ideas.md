   Why they care: Limited income and strict monthly budgets.
   Solution concept: Live cart total that updates as items are scanned or added.
   Likelihood: 9/10

2. Unsure Which Product Is Healthier
   Problem: Nutrition labels are confusing and time-consuming to read.
   Why they care: Want to eat healthier without becoming an expert.
   Solution concept: Simple color-coded health score when scanning an item.
   Likelihood: 8/10

3. Forgetting Items at Home
   Problem: Student realizes they already have an item after buying it.
   Why they care: Wastes money and fridge space.
   Solution concept: Pantry inventory synced with past purchases and reminders.
   Likelihood: 7/10

4. Overwhelmed by Too Many Choices
   Problem: Too many brands/options slow decision-making.
   Why they care: Grocery trips take too long.
   Solution concept: “Best pick for you” recommendation based on preferences.
   Likelihood: 8/10

5. Trouble Comparing Prices Across Sizes
   Problem: Unit pricing is small and easy to miss.
   Why they care: Want best value for money.
   Solution concept: Automatic unit price comparison after scanning items.
   Likelihood: 9/10

6. Impulse Buying Junk Food
   Problem: Student adds snacks they didn’t plan to buy.
   Why they care: Affects health and budget.
   Solution concept: Gentle nudge: “Not on your list—still want this?”
   Likelihood: 7/10

7. Unsure What Meals They Can Make
   Problem: Has ingredients but no meal ideas.
   Why they care: Ends up ordering takeout instead.
   Solution concept: Recipe suggestions based on items in cart.
   Likelihood: 8/10

8. Long Checkout Lines
   Problem: Waiting wastes time between classes or work.
   Why they care: Tight schedules.
   Solution concept: Scan-and-go checkout with phone payment.
   Likelihood: 9/10

9. Difficulty Tracking Dietary Restrictions
   Problem: Accidentally buys food with allergens or unwanted ingredients.
   Why they care: Health and comfort.
   Solution concept: Allergen warnings when scanning items.
   Likelihood: 8/10

10. Forgetting Sales or Deals
    Problem: Misses discounts that could save money.
    Why they care: Stretch limited grocery budget.
    Solution concept: Auto-alerts for deals on items in their list.
    Likelihood: 8/10
