# INTERVIEW SCRIPT

A) Intro
“Thanks for chatting with me. I’m trying to understand how people keep track of things they want to do and how they actually turn those ideas into plans.”
Warm-up question:
“Can you tell me a little about your lifestyle and how you usually spend your free time?”

B) Context + Baseline (5 minutes)
B) Context + Baseline
Goal: understand their relationship to inspiration and time.
“Walk me through a typical week outside of work or class.”
“How do you usually decide what to do when you have free time?”
“Do you actively plan fun things, or does it happen more spontaneously?”
“Where do ideas for things to do usually come from?”
Reflection prompts:
“When you say you’re ‘too busy,’ what does that actually look like?”
“Tell me more about that.”
“What happened next?”

C) Story Time (core section: 15–25 minutes)
Ask for a real recent example.
“Tell me about the last time you saw something online and thought, ‘I want to do that.’”
Dig:
Where did you save it?
Did you tell anyone?
Did you schedule it?
What happened after that?
Then:
“Tell me about the last time you tried to plan something with friends.”
What triggered it?
How did coordination happen?
What was frustrating?
How long did it take?
Did it actually happen?
If not, why?
Frequency + severity:
“How often do plans fall through?”
“How often do saved ideas just sit there?”
“What’s the emotional cost when that happens?”

D) Current Workarounds

“Where do you store ideas today?”
“Do you ever revisit your saved posts?”
“How do you check availability with friends?”
“Do you use Google Calendar socially?”

Follow-up:

“What do you like about how you handle this?”
“What feels messy or fragmented?”
“Have you tried Notion? Notes? Partiful? Shared calendars? Why or why not?”
E) Ideal Outcome

“If you could magically become someone who actually did the things you saved, what would be different?”
“What would an ideal planning experience feel like?”
“Would you rather plan in advance or have smart suggestions when you’re free?”
“What would make something feel effortless instead of draining?”

F) Wrap-Up
“What advice would you give someone designing a tool to help people follow through on their interests?”
“What would make you ignore it completely?”

4) Follow-Up Question Bank (Customized)
Push deeper:
“What makes planning feel like work?”
“When do you feel most socially fulfilled?”
“When do you feel disconnected?”
“What role does your calendar play in your personal life?”
Avoid leading:
Instead of:
“Would you use an app that does X?”
Ask:
“What would need to exist for this problem to feel solved?”
“What would make you trust something to manage your time socially?”
“What concerns would you have about syncing calendars?”

WARM-UP (2 min)
"Thanks so much for taking the time. I'm a Columbia student working on a class project, and I'm trying to understand how people keep track of things they want to do — like restaurants, events, activities — and how they actually turn those ideas into plans with friends. I'm not selling anything — I genuinely want to learn from your experience. There are no right or wrong answers."
[If recording] "Would it be OK if I record this for my notes? I won't share it outside our team."

CONTEXT / SCREENING (2 min)
"To start — can you tell me a little about yourself? What does a typical week look like for you outside of work or school?"
"How would you describe your social life right now — do you feel like you're doing the things you actually want to do, or is there a gap?"

PROBLEM DISCOVERY (8–10 min)
"Think about the last time you saw something online — a restaurant, an exhibit, a hike, whatever — and thought, I want to do that. What happened after that moment?" → "Where did you save it?" → "Did you tell anyone about it?" → "Did it ever actually happen? Why or why not?"


"Now walk me through the last time you tried to make plans with friends. How did it start, and how did the coordination actually play out?" → "What was the most annoying part?" → "How long did it take from idea to confirmed plan?" → "Did it actually happen, or did it fall apart? What went wrong?"


"How often would you say plans fall through or saved ideas just sit there untouched?" → "How does that feel when it happens? Is it a small thing or does it actually bother you?"


"Right now, what are you using to keep track of ideas or coordinate with friends? Notes app, group chats, saved posts, Google Calendar, anything else?" → "What do you like about how you're handling it?" → "What feels messy or broken about it?" → "Have you tried anything like Partiful, shared calendars, Notion, or anything purpose-built for this? What happened?"



DEPTH QUESTIONS (3–5 min — pick 1–2 based on the conversation)
"If nothing changed and you kept doing things the way you do now for the next year, what would that feel like?"
"If you could magically become someone who actually followed through on the things you saved and wanted to do — what would be different about your life?"
"What would an ideal planning experience feel like? Would you want to plan ahead, or would you rather something just surfaced the right idea at the right moment?"
"Who in your friend group is usually the one organizing plans? What's that dynamic like?"

WRAP-UP (2 min)
"This has been really helpful. Is there anything about how you plan, save ideas, or coordinate with people that I should've asked about but didn't?"
"Do you know one or two other people who deal with this same kind of thing — saving stuff they never get to, struggling to lock in plans — who might be willing to chat with me?"
"Thanks so much — I really appreciate your time."
