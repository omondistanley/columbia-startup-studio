## User Interview Guide Template 

# Problem Space

People with busy schedules who struggle with scheduling events with friends, family, etc. 
There is a gap between what people say they want to do and what actually gets scheduled into their lives. Ideas are captured everywhere, but without coordination and calendar integration, they rarely become real experiences.

# Research Question
Primary research question:
	How do socially active young professionals currently keep track of things they want to do and coordinate plans with friends, and what makes it hard to actually follow through?
Secondary questions (optional):
	What triggers the idea to “do something” (TikTok, friend suggestion, boredom, seasonal change)?
	Where do those ideas get stored?
	What causes those ideas to be forgotten?
	How do they currently coordinate with friends?
	What makes planning feel annoying or draining?
	What does a “successful week” socially or personally look like to them?
	When do they feel most aligned with their interests?

# Target Users
Who counts as a target user?
	Digitally active people who:
	Save content about experiences (restaurants, exhibits, walks, events)
Value social connection
	Use Google Calendar
	Feel like they “never get around to” things they want to do
Inclusion Criteria:
	Uses social media regularly (TikTok, IG, etc.)
	Saves or screenshots things they want to try
	Has at least 2–3 close friends they try to see semi-regularly
Uses Google Calendar
	Expresses feeling “busy” but still wanting meaningful experiences
Exclusion Criteria:
	Rarely makes plans socially
	Doesn’t use any calendar system
	Prefers full spontaneity and dislikes pre-planning entirely
	Has extremely rigid schedules (e.g., shift workers with no flexibility)
Other Stakeholders
	Friends (whose schedules need to align)
	Partners
	Roommates
	Event hosts
	Group chat participants
	Social planners within friend groups (“the one who always organizes”)
	Calendar platforms (Google ecosystem)
	Possibly mental health / wellness stakeholders

2) Assumptions → Questions (do this before you start)
Your assumptions (write bluntly)
Assumption 1:
 People save way more ideas than they actually execute.
Assumption 2:
 The main reason ideas don’t happen is scheduling friction with friends.
Assumption 3:
 People want help organizing their interests, but they don’t want it to feel like another productivity app.
Convert each assumption into open-ended questions
Assumption 1 becomes:
“Tell me about the last few things you saved that you haven’t done yet. What happened?”
Assumption 2 becomes:
“Walk me through the last time you tried to plan something with friends and it didn’t work out.”
Assumption 3 becomes:
“Tell me about tools you’ve used to stay organized socially or personally. What felt helpful, and what felt overwhelming?”
