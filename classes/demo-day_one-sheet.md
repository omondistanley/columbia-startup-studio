# Demo Day One-Sheet: 5-Minute MVP Presentation Guide

**What this is:** A guide for your 5-minute demo presentation on March 12.

**Format:** A couple quick slides to set up the problem, then live demo of your core workflow.

---

## Slide 1: Team + Product Name (30 sec)
- Team name, members, product name
- One-sentence description of what you're building
- Keep this fast

## Slide 2: The Problem (60 sec)
- Who has this problem? Be specific (not "everyone")
- What's the pain? What do they do today?
- Evidence: one strong quote or stat from your interviews
- This is your setup; make the audience feel the problem

## Slide 3: Your Solution (30 sec)
- One sentence: what is it and how does it work?
- Transition to live demo: "Let me show you"

---

## Live Demo: Core Workflow (3 min)

**This is the main event.** Show, don't tell.

- Walk through the user journey from first touch to **aha moment**
- The aha moment = the point where the user gets the core value
- Use real (or realistic) data, not lorem ipsum
- If something is faked/hardcoded, that's fine; just show the experience
- Narrate as you go: "A user lands here, they do X, and then they see Y"

---

## What NOT to Do

- Don't spend 3 minutes on slides and 2 minutes rushing through the demo
- Don't show every feature; show the ONE path that matters
- Don't apologize for what's not built yet; show what IS built
- Don't read bullet points off slides

---

**The goal:** By the end, the audience should understand the problem AND have seen the product solve it. That's it.
